Using the App

The Phishing Pole offers two ways for users to scan emails for potentional phishing scams.

1. Manuel Input (reccommended for the non-tech savvy)

To use manuel input, simply copy and paste who the email you recieved was sent from.

This should look like "Example@example.com" or another variation.
Optionally, you may upload the content of the email itself, but it is not required.

2. Upload (more indepth analysis)

To use upload, you must download the email in either a .txt or .eml file, then upload to the tool for an analysis.

FAQ

A Phishing email is a type of internet scam in which an attacker trys to trick a victim into thinking a fraudulent email is legitimate.

The Phishing Pole works by collecting information from emails to help determine legitimacy.
We examine:
- The sender email.
- The content of the email.
- The email security headers.
We feed this information to an algorithm, which determines the likeihood of a scam email.

The Phishing Pole temporarily stores uploaded emails for examination. Emails are deleted after examination is complete.

No security tool is 100% foolproof against scams.
The Phishing Pole can be a helpful tool to discover scams, but conduct your own independant assessment for validation.
